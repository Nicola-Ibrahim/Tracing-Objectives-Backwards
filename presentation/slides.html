Hadamard’s Well-Posedness

1. Existence

Does a solution \( x \) exist for target \( y^* \)?

CORE ISSUE

2. Uniqueness

Is there only ONE solution? (The One-to-Many problem)

3. Stability

Stability is rare in complex inverse landscapes.

"Uniqueness is the biggest hurdle. Because many designs can produce the same result, a model that only gives one answer—or an average—is flawed."
Part II • Slide 3

The Core Problem: Ill-Posedness

\[ f^{-1}(y^*) \rightarrow \{x_1, x_2, \dots\} \]

Inverse is Ill-Posed. Mapping from \( y \to x \) is ambiguous.

Information Loss

Forward process 'collapses' features.

Ambiguity

One result maps to countless configurations.

Part II • Slide 4

The "Average" Trap

Regression Fails Inverse Problems

MSE loss attempts to find the mean design. In physics, the mean is often invalid.

\[ \hat{x} = E[x|y] \quad \text{but} \quad f(\hat{x}) \neq y^* \]

"The average of a tuxedo and a swimsuit is neither formal nor waterproof."

Part III • Slide 1

Target-Driven Inverse Exploration

Objective

Specifying target \( y^* \) and receiving feasible candidates \( \{\hat{x}_k\} \).

Innovation

Moving from single-point optimization to distribution exploration.

Utility

A "menu" of valid options for the designer, not a black box.

Part III • Slide 3

The Propose-Check Workflow

Step 1

PROPOSE

Sample diverse candidates from \( q_\theta(x|y^*) \)

Step 2

CHECK

Verify validity via forward surrogate \( E(\hat{x}) \)

Step 3

RANK

Select best-shot or multiple diverse designs

Part III • Slide 4

Probabilistic Engines

MDN

Mixture Density

Models multiple Gaussian "peaks" to define distinct solution islands.

\( \sum \pi_k \mathcal{N}(\mu_k, \Sigma_k) \)

CVAE

Conditional VAE

Uses latent variable \( z \) to navigate the design manifold.

\( z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \)

INN

Invertible NN

Bijective mapping preserving all information across directions.

\( (x) \leftrightarrow (y, z) \)
